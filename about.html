I'm currently a student at Cornell University who is passionate about biological research. I love to learn, and nothing in science inspires me more than learning the way that life functions.

Biology + Computer Science = Computational Biology

I have always been fascinated by wildlife. My local zoo was my playground growing up, and it sparked a love for animals. Unfortunately, I can't stand the sight of blood, so becoming a veterinarian was out of question. Therefore, I've decided to focus on ecology and conservation, trying to protect the world and its lifeforms as best I can.

As you can tell by me coding my own website, I love to program. Unlike biology, computer science has not always been a passion of mine. In fact, I hated it in middle school. It wasn't until I got to high school and was forced to take a class that I began to explore it again. The first few tries were still just as tough and frustrating, but after I got over the initial learning curve of programming syntax and logic, I started to love it. Problem solving is more satisfying to me than slime videos. My favorite part of programming is the debugging process because I get to find solutions and see everything work out.

I've combined my two academic passions by pursuing computational biology. I'm most inspired by big impact research. My lofty goal is to have a positive impact.
